The Future of Web Development

GeeksforGeeks blog

This week I explored the future of Web Development. I'm interested in how the field will change in the next few years and what new technologies or methods will be on the rise. The stage of web development has changed rapidly over the past 20 years, and it's exciting to think about where it will be in the next 20.

A resource I looked at was this blog from GeeksforGeeks. The blog identifies six different technologies or trends that will soon take over the development forefront.

  • Artificial intelligence: Artificial intelligence is currently being used by major companies, and soon this technology will be more widely available for sites to implement.
  • Internet of Things: I was a bit confused why this topic was brought up, but after reading into it further it made more sense. The internet of things refers to the millions of devices connected to the internet. These devices all collect and send data over the network and does not require human interaction. This is related to web development because soon web develpers will need to provide applciations that display and interpret data collected from these devices. One connection I thought of that relates to this point is new popular devices such as Fitbits and other fitness tracking mechanisms. The devices are great, but they are practically useless without the ability to display meaningful information to the user through an interface. This is what I thought of when reading it, and I think the rise of new devices will require web developers to gain a more complex understanding of the physical structure of the devices, how to collect and make sense of the data, and creating a user interfaces that support the data.
  • Virtual Reality: Virtual Reality has definitely been on the rise in recent years. New headsets available to the public accompanied by VR applications has contributed to this boom. Some companies have already taken advantage of VR capability by doing virtual tours of apartments and even simulating products from a business in the real world (though that's AR). VR capability in web applications will definitely increase more within the next few years.
  • 360 degree video: Another aspect that will be included in websites is 360 video. It provides users with a more interactive view and will probably get more attention than regular photos/videos. In my VR/AR class, we used 360 video cameras to shoot a movie... it was pretty hard. You have to position the camera very carefully to truly put something at the center of the shot because you can see so much at once. It will be interesting to see which content will be captured using 360 cameras.
  • Voice Command: The blog predicts that voice command technology will continue to expand in the development of web applications.
  • Motion User Interface: The last trend or technology that this blog predicts will rise is motion design. I've seen recently that business are starting to implement this technology, and it's definitely more attractive to customers.
